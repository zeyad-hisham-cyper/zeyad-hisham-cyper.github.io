// About

Who I Am

I build things that talk to the physical world — then I figure out how to break them.

Electronics and Communications Engineer by degree, embedded systems practitioner by obsession, and a cybersecurity student by the logical conclusion that if you understand how systems are built from the ground up, you have a serious advantage in understanding how they fail. That progression is not a career pivot. It is the same curiosity, applied deeper.

Ranked first among my peers across three consecutive years at Misr International University — not because I memorized the most, but because I could not stop asking why things work the way they do. I have shipped hardware, written firmware, trained ML models, co-founded a robotics startup that reached 800+ students, and published research that won a best paper award before I finished high school. I graduate in 2026 and I am actively looking for the next hard problem.

If you are a recruiter, team lead, or founder reading this: I am the engineer who reads the datasheet on a Friday night because it is genuinely interesting. That combination of hardware depth and security direction is rare. You probably already know that.

Door Locker Security System

Password-based entry system with persistent credential storage in EEPROM and PIR-based intrusion detection. Dual-MCU architecture over UART: one unit handles the user interface, the other manages the lock mechanism and security logic. Designed to demonstrate layered hardware security at the embedded level.

Embedded

AF Function Generator

Analog function generator producing square, triangle, and sine waveforms from a single astable multivibrator core. The square wave feeds a non-inverting integrator to produce the triangle wave, which passes through an RC low-pass filter to yield a smooth sine output. Designed, simulated in Proteus, and fabricated as a PCB prototype.
