About

Who I Am

I'm an electronics and communications engineer with a strong foundation in embedded systems, microcontroller programming, and IoT architecture. I spent several years designing firmware for resource-constrained devices, working across RTOS environments, communication protocols (UART, SPI, I²C, MQTT), and low-level hardware bring-up — so I understand the full stack from schematic to deployed system.

Today my focus has shifted to cybersecurity — specifically the intersection of hardware security, network defence, and applied machine learning for threat detection. I approach security problems the way an engineer would: by understanding the system deeply before trying to break or harden it. I'm currently pursuing certifications and building tools that bridge both worlds.
